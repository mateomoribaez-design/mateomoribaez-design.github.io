2f.

The variable of interest is normally distributed because the shape of the distribution falls on the reference line in the QQ plot. Not only does the general shape align with the reference line, but the few outliers in the individual lobster weights can be seen as not significant enough to indicate skewness, accepting the data as normally distributed. Both “Marine Protected Areas” and “not protected” groups show these patterns.

2g.

Using the F-test result (\(p = 0.29\)), there is no significant difference in the variance of lobster weights between the Marine Protected Area and non-protected sites, so equal variance can be assumed between the two data sets. This means that the spread of data points around the mean are roughly the same across the two groups, backing up the claim of there being no significant difference.

var.test(weight_lbs ~ protection_type, # compared the variance of the two normally distributed samples, seeing if they are equal or not.

2i.

  • Using a t-test is appropriate for this situation because we are comparing mean lobster weights between two independent groups (MPA and non-protected) using a continuous response variable.

  • Normal distribution was evaluated through the data points following the reference in the QQ plot, and homogeneity of variance was assessed by using an F-test to compare two variances.

2j.

We found a significant difference in mean lobster weight between MPA (n = 35) and non-protected areas (n = 30) (Students t-test, t(63) = -10.08, p < 0.001, a = 0.05).
